# Work Mechanics

## Core Type
Burst worker with high performance in flow states and low performance outside of them.

---

## Energy Dependency
- Strong dependence on mood and energy
- Low ability to force productivity without internal drive

### Implication
Work should be started through small actions, not motivation.

---

## Motivation System
Driven by:
- Visible progress
- Completion satisfaction (ego + relief)

Lost by:
- Stagnation
- Long unclear problems

---

## Failure Pattern
1. Starts fast
2. Builds momentum
3. Encounters blocker
4. Attempts resolution
5. If unresolved → delay
6. If repeated → abandonment

---

## Anti-Failure Strategy
- Limit stuck time (~1 hour)
- Switch approach quickly
- Use external help early

---

## Overengineering Behavior
- Aware of overengineering in real time
- Continues if it feels beneficial

### Rule
Allow overengineering only after a working version exists

---

## Flow Trigger
Triggered by:
- Fast feedback
- Perceived competence
- Early success

Broken by:
- Unclear next step
- Lack of visible progress

---

## Optimal Work Mode
- Short, intense bursts
- Iterative progress
- Frequent visible milestones

---

## Project Constraints
- Max ~3 parallel projects
- Must either finish or consciously abandon

---

## Assistance
External help significantly improves efficiency.

### Rule
- Use help early
- Understand solution at least superficially

---

## Summary
- Progress is critical
- Stagnation must be minimized
- Completion is the main reward driver
- Work system must support bursts, not consistency
